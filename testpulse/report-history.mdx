# Report History

Every report you generate is automatically saved to your personal history inside Azure DevOps. Your history is tied to your ADO account and synced across browsers.

## Accessing your history

Click the **History** tab in TestPulse. Your reports are listed in reverse chronological order.

## What the history shows

Each entry displays:

- Test plan name
- Generation date and time
- Report title
- Results summary (passed / failed / total)
- Pass rate badge (green / orange / red based on your thresholds)

Click a row to expand it and see full project details, tester, approver, and per-outcome counts.

## Reloading a report

Click **Load** on any history entry to restore the report data and metadata into the Report tab. This lets you re-export in a different format or review the results without re-fetching from ADO.

<Note>
  Reloading a report does not re-fetch data from ADO. It restores the snapshot that was saved at generation time.
</Note>

## Comparing two reports

Select any two reports using the checkboxes on the left, then click **Compare**.

The comparison dialog shows:

- **Metrics diff** — pass rate, execution rate, passed/failed/blocked delta
- **Changed tests** — test cases whose outcome changed between the two reports (with Improved / Regressed badge)
- **Added tests** — test cases present in the newer report but not the older
- **Removed tests** — test cases present in the older report but not the newer

Click **Export HTML** to download a standalone comparison report.

## Adding notes

Expand any history entry and use the **Notes** field to add context (max 500 characters). Notes are saved automatically when you click **Save**.

Useful for: explaining why a test failed, linking to a related change, or marking a report as the official sprint deliverable.

## Exporting history to CSV

Click **Export CSV** in the history header to download all your reports as a spreadsheet. Includes: plan, date, title, passed, total, pass rate.

## Storage usage

History is stored in your personal ADO Extension Data quota (5 MB). A storage indicator at the top of the history page shows current usage.

<Warning>
  When storage exceeds 80%, the indicator turns red. Delete old reports you no longer need to free up space.
</Warning>

## Deleting a report

Click the **trash icon** on any row to permanently delete that history entry. This action cannot be undone.
